Personal Statement

I am a master's student with a passion for molecular dynamics and structural biology, with plans to persue a doctorate combining structural biology and bioinformatics. Currently, I am working on projects that allow undergraduate chemistry and biochemistry students to learn about structural biology and protein-ligand interactions in easy-to-use Jupyter Notebooks. As my bachelor's degree is in biochemistry, I have experience with both "wet lab" and "dry lab" techniques.

Research and Relevant Experience

Rochester Institute of Technology

Graduate Research Assistant - Craig Lab (Summer 2024)

Researching and developing Jupyter notebooks with Python code for binding small molecules (ligands) to proteins, with goals of converting them to web applications with a simple user interface.

Graduate Research - Craig Lab (Spring 2024 to Present)

Researching and developing Jupyter notebooks with python code and libraries that model protein-ligand interactions and docking, with the docking positions and binding locations of each ligand being analyzed using machine learning. Eventually, these notebooks will be converted into web applications with a simple user interface, designed to be utilized by undergraduate students in chemistry and biochemistry.

Undergraduate Research - Craig Lab (Spring 2024)

Working with public datasets and information from sites including but not limited to NCBI and PDB and integrating python to automate repetitive tasks using MolSSI practices, with a focus on molecular mechanics modeling.

Undergraduate Research - Gleghorn Lab (Spring 2023 to Spring 2024)

Determiniation of the binding position of a cyclometalated planar ligand on a G-Quadruplex using X-ray crystallography
